    • Amutha Kannan

    Amutha Kannan is the founder and chief curator of The Content Curators. This is a first-of-its-kind professional content writing service in Coimbatore. The Content Curators creates and curates content to help institutions and organisations add value to their thought leadership and brand building. The team consolidates, compiles, organises and aligns persuasively and honestly the content provided by organisations to give them that winning edge. Amutha is also an independent journalist / writer, and a media resource person for colleges and universities. She has been a panellist on various conferences, conducted workshops for students and faculty, and has been instrumental in formulating journalism programme in some colleges. She has a BA in Sociology from Stella Maris College, Chennai, MA in Journalism and Mass Communication from University of Madras, and an LLB from Karnatak University. She has had a stint in corporate communication in Larsen & Toubro Limited, Chennai, and has been a correspondent in the Coimbatore bureau of The Hindu before becoming a freelance journalist. She has also contributed to web news portals such as The News Minute, The Covai Post and Simplicity. Amutha has written extensively on higher education, social welfare, agriculture, women, children, disability, people living with HIV/AIDS, geriatrics, and tribal communities. During her stint at The Hindu, she received the ‘Laadli Media Award for Gender Sensitivity 2011-12’, supported by the United Nations Population Fund, in the human interest category for her story titled “Ensuring Dignified Burial for the Destitute” where she wrote on three women who buried unclaimed bodies. She is a TED speaker and has delivered two talks. Her first talk in 2013 as Principal Correspondent of The Hindu was on the topic “Dignity in Life, Dignity in Death” based on the story that fetched her the Laadli Media Award. The other one in 2015 as a freelance journalist was on “Defence: Living Life beyond the Ordinary”, based on her experiences as a Defence wife having lived in conflict / civil unrest areas in Kashmir and Assam. Amutha is married to an Air Force Officer and has a daughter and a son.
